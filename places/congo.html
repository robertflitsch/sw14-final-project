Congo Free State

Perfectly exemplifying the dark underbelly of New Imperialism, the Congo Free State, controlled by King Leopold II of Belgium, was founded in 1877. Since this acquisition occurred around the same time as Britain's involvement in the Suez, other European nations panicked and called the Berlin Conference in order to stake their own claims.

Henry Morton Stanley, a Briton famous for charting Africa and searching for David Livingstone, helped claim the Congo for King Leopold. This attached Britain's name to the brutal administration of the colony and inspired Joseph Conrad to write Heart of Darkness. The Congo Free State illustrated the sharp contrast between Britain's former Empire of Liberty and the harsh institution of New Imperialism.
