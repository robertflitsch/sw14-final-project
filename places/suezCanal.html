Suez Canal

The British involvement in the construction of the Suez Canal in Egypt precipitated the Scramble for Africa. The construction of the canal was an important step toward uniting the empire and facilitating free trade for the Britons. Though locals began to build the canal in 1869, the British bought a majority share in the project in 1875.

In 1882, an Egyptian uprising occurred against the regime backed by the British. The British responded by deploying a "temporary" force of troops. A religious uprising in Sudan occurred soon after, further entrenching Britain in Africa. The idea of a domain from "Cape to Cairo" crept into being, setting off a chain reaction and prompting the Berlin Conference. Other European nations feared an Africa dominated by the British and opted to throw their hats in the ring.
