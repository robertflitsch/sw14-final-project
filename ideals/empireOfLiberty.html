Empire of Liberty

From around 1830 to 1860, the British considered themselves the keepers of an empire of liberty. Their goal in expansion was to bring the freedom and opportunity of England to the masses. Their subjects were treated benignly and justly.

However, as this era progressed, the trend erred more towards empire and less towards liberty. Even during the so-called reign of liberty, many problems of revolt, famine and discontent lurked beneath the surface. The Sepoy Rebellion occurred during this period.
