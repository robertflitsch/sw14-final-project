Cape to Cairo

Many Britons envisioned an empire in Africa stretching from "Cape to Cairo." This would make the English the leader of a continent occupied by many European countries. The other powers, fearing this, held the Berlin Conference to ensure fair divisions. Still, the British held on to their vision as an ideal. Cecil Rhodes was one of the main supporters of this cause and used his personal wealth and power to further a "Cape to Cairo" mission.
