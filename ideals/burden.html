The White Man's Burden

"The White Man's Burden," by Rudyard Kipling, is a poem which can be viewed from two angles, either celebrating or satirizing the racism exhibited towards conquered peoples in the era of New Imperialism. It is either noble (or ironic) to adopt a patronizing attitude to the "savages" in India and Africa and civilize them accordingly. The poem was originally written for Victoria's Diamond Jubilee, the selection was replaced at the last minute. It is still used to characterize the attitudes of the period.
