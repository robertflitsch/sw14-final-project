New Imperialism

Characterized by a vital expansion of oversees territory by Western nations, this period occurred during the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries. For Britain, this trend had major economic impacts, marking a definitive shift from a mercantile to a truly capitalist economy. Socially, racist and anglocenric attitudes largely define this period, contrasting it to an "Empire of Liberty." British citizens were encouraged to "take up the white man's burden" and civilize the savages now included in the British domain. Much of New Imperialism was driven by competition. This competition for land, power and resources came to a head during the "Scramble for Africa."
