Joseph Chamberlain

Joseph Chamberlain worked his way up from humble beginnings to become one of the most influential statesmen of the industrial era, though he never held the position of Prime Minister. He was a proponent of social reform and worked to spread public education, and make utilities more widely available. He wanted to bridge the gap between the classes. He was a member of the liberal party, but he eventually became more conservative, especially in terms of supporting New Imperialism. He championed imperial unity and envisioned an empire held together by a cohesive government and a high, protective tariff to uphold free trade.
