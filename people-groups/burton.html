Richard Burton

A brilliant linguist, Richard Burton adapted to the cultures he visited and obtained a more accurate picture of the empire than most of his contemporaries. He began his career as a captain for the British East India Company and later transformed into an explorer for the Royal Geographical society and was knighted. This shift in employer from a private company to the crown paints Burton as a microcosm of the empire itself. It mirrors the shift from Empire of Liberty to New Imperialism.

Burton disguised as Arabic and made a hajj to Mecca, worked in India and explored the great lakes of Africa, acting, also as a geographic tie between the domains of the empire.
