British East India Company

The decline of the East India Company was both economic and political. It was brought about by the fiscal costs of The Sepoy Rebellion, and by Queen Victoria and the parliament's decision to increase imperial involvement and promote free trade by opening the industry to smaller merchants.

The East India Company, founded in 1600, was a symbol of the old empire. It was a reflection of the Empire of Liberty insofar as it displayed the power an individual entity could possess independent of its country of origin. The decline of this powerful force came in tandem with an increase in direct government. It marked a transition point into New Imperialism.
