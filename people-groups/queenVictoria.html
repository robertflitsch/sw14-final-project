Queen Victoria

As a long-reigning monarch and proponent of imperial power, Victoria was a symbol for the British people and empire. Calling herself Empress of India, she showed that her titles were not restricted to mainland Britain. She truly strove to govern the entire realm, exhibiting curiosity for and interest in her foreign domains.

Queen Victoria's Diamond Jubilee in 1897 is the perfect example of her place in the picture of empire and how her reign saw a transition between an Empire of Liberty and New Imperialism. She was a true politician who took an active role in the workings of empire.
