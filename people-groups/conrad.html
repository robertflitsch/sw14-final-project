Joseph Conrad

Jospeh Conrad was born to a Polish family in 1857. Though he did not learn English until he was an adult, he became one of its premier novelists. His most famous work, Heart of Darkness, depicts the dark side of colonization and the movement of New Imperialism by following an English captain commissioned by the Belgians in the Congo.

Conrad was able to bring the horrors of the imperial effort to the masses and make people question the Cape to Cairo sentiment with his stories. However he also dehumanized Africans and furthered racist ideologies characterized in "The White Man's Burden."
