David Livingstone

David Livingstone became a symbol of imperial power and moral fortitude for the British people. Though Livingstone strongly supported the imperial effort, he very much stood at the crossroads between old and new, bridging the gap between the Empire of Liberty and New Imperialism. Though he wanted to spread the freedom of Britain to the masses, he was deeply racist and took up the mantle of conqueror, acting as a missionary and explorer in South Africa and promoting Britain's economic interests in the form of free trade.

In context with Livingstone's contemporaries, Richard Burton and Henry Morton Stanley, he best embodied the morality and patriotism of empire. He discovered Victoria Falls and named them after his beloved Queen [Victoria]. Livingston is charged with bringing stories of Africa to the common Brits, educating them on the lands he traveled to, but also instilling racism and easing the nation into New Imperialism.
