Berlin Conference

The Berlin Conference marked an undoing of the African continent. The major European powers met in Berlin to divide up the area amongst themselves. These divisions were not made across tribal lines, but rather drew arbitrary boundaries and caused problems. After King Leopold II created the Congo Free State, other Europeans wanted a stake in the land. The Berlin Conference precipitated the Scramble for Africa to ensure that the continent was "fairly" divided between these superpowers.
