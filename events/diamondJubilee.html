Diamond Jubilee

The Diamond Jubilee in 1897 was a celebration of Victoria's sixty years on the throne. The event was a lavish display of power featuring a procession of troops and peoples from across the vast area in British control. Whereas years before, these dominions may not have been as present in the Victorian mind, during this celebration, they were at the forefront. Though powers from India, Africa and the other stretches of the empire were displayed, they were also seen as oddities, and their forces were indisputably inferior to the British army. Despite inclusion in the domain, the foreign lands of the empire were targeting with racism and close-mindedness in the spirit of taking up "The White Man's Burden."
